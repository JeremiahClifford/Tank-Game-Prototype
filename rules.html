The Rules

Game Rules

  • Every player is a tank on a 37 x 37 board

  • Each player starts with 3 (three) health at a random position on the board

  • Each player receives 1 (one) action point

  • Players can use an action point to move 1 (one) space

  • Players can use an action point to shoot any other player that is within range

  • Players can send an action point to any other player

  • Shooting a player reduces their health by 1 (one)

  • Any player that reaches 0 (zero) health dies

  • Dead players in the jury

  • Jury members can vote on 1 (one) player to recieve an extra action point

  • After every interval, when action points are given out, the living player that received the most
    jury votes gets 1 extra action point

  • The last player left alive is the winner

Controls

  • Click anywhere on the board to select that grid square, you will see a context menu of information

  • If the square has a player in it, you will see information about that player and buttons with actions
    that you can take in regards to that player

  • If the square that you select is where you are, you will see different buttons with different actions

  • Press any of these buttons to take those actions

  • If you do not see any buttons, it is because you do not have any action points and thus can not take any
    actions

  • When you click the move button, you will see a bunch of orange squares in the spaces around you.
    Those are the spaces that you can move to. Clicking on any of those squares will move you to that square

  • When you click the shoot button, you will see that some of the other players have a red square
    around them. These are the players that you can shoot. Clicking on any of them will shoot them

  • Right click anywhere on the board to close the context menu

  • If you are dead you will see a list of the alive players that you can select and a vote button. Selecting
    a player from the list and pressing the vote button submits a vote for that player to get the extra
    action point. Submitting a second vote overwrites your previous votes
